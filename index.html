Welcome! My name is Haikuo Li. I am a Ph.D. student in Bioscience at Washington University in St. Louis School of Medicine.
As a first-year student in the Division of Biology & Biomedical Sciences (Program in Molecular Genetics and Genomics), I am currently rotating in the lab of Prof. Sid Puram studying tumor heterogeneity using single-cell RNA sequencing. See Graduate to learn more about my graduate studies.


I got my Bachelor of Science Degree at Shanghai Jiao Tong University (Bioscience, Zhiyuan Honors Program) in 2019. At Shanghai Center for Systems Biology, I led a proteomic project on identification of lymphoma classifiers via mass spectrometry.
Moreover, I spent my fourth year as a visiting student at Yale University Department of Immunobiology. We used protein engineering to modulate the immunotherapeutic efficacy of Interleukin-18 by protein engineering. See Undergraduate to learn more about my undergraduate studies.
